Experiment 3 - Generative Methods

Imitate

I started off with the base code example M_1_5_03 that I found on generative-gestaltung.de (Link: http://www.generative-gestaltung.de/2/sketches/?02_M/M_1_5_03).

I thought that the piece was captivating so I started playing around with the values in the source code. Soon I found that every element of the supposedly randomly generated lines could be modified in any way that I pleased. I started changing values relating to the noise and amount of agents, and was pleasantly surprised by the subsequent changes that followed. From this point on, after understanding most of the parameters I wanted to try and create my own sketch using the code.

Integrate

After playing around with the code a bit, I’d created quite a few sketches of a similar matter. Many of them didn’t have any rhyme or reason to them, but there were a few that really stuck to me in their resemblance to real life images. I likened one to sparks flying after a chinese fireworks festival.

I found that the parameter controlling the noise’s strength determined the direction that the “agents” would adopt. The closer that the strength was to 0, the more horizontal the agents would travel across the screen. Seeing this, I altered the colors and agents to simulate something similar to shooting stars, and it worked.

Innovate

After more experimentation, I was deciding on what I wanted to create for the experiment. Although I was already kind of making the experiment I wasn’t happy with the sketches I’d made thus far. I looked outside of the section classroom to see the rain droplets falling along the window outside, and realized what I wanted to make.

I used an RGB color picker to determine the sky and rain colors that I wanted, and after that I altered the size of the droplets to what seemed right. I almost felt as if the piece felt done, but then for the fun of it I added a body of water below the rain to finish it off. Seeing the droplets go into the water body changes their color as well.

Reflection
